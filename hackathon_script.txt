SENSLY — 3-MINUTE HACKATHON PITCH SCRIPT
=========================================
For use with ChatGPT or any AI presentation assistant.
Tone: Infomercial energy. Punchy. Real. Fast.

---

[HOOK — 0:00–0:20]

You know that feeling when you walk into a crowded restaurant and your brain just... shuts down?

The fluorescent lights. The noise. The chaos.

For 1 in 5 people — people with autism, ADHD, PTSD, sensory processing disorder — that's not just uncomfortable. That's a crisis.

And right now? There's no app for that.

Until Sensly.

---

[THE PROBLEM — 0:20–0:35]

Neurodivergent people spend enormous energy just figuring out if a place is safe to enter.

They can't Google "is this coffee shop too loud for me."
They can't filter a map by "low sensory load."
They're flying blind — every single time.

---

[THE SOLUTION — 0:35–1:10]

Sensly is a crowdsourced sensory environment app.

Open it up. Your phone's microphone is already measuring the noise level — live, in decibels, right now. Sound. Motion. Light. Temperature. Four sensors, one screen.

Your axolotl mascot tells you at a glance: green means calm. Red means get out.

Tap the Map tab. Every venue around you has a sensory score — color-coded pins, community-rated for noise, crowding, lighting, and predictability. Tap a pin, see the full breakdown. Tap Rate — and your 30-second mic measurement gets added to the community database automatically.

That's the flywheel. Every user makes the map better for the next one.

---

[THE DEPTH — 1:10–1:40]

But Sensly isn't just a map.

Set up your profile — tell us your triggers, your diagnosis, your sensory preferences. The app learns what matters to you specifically.

The Calm tab is a 4-phase sensory intervention: guided breathing, a personalized tool picker — headphones, deep pressure, grounding exercises — all ranked by what your profile says actually helps you. Walk in stressed. Walk out calm. Sensly logs the moment.

The Journal tab shows your week at a glance — a risk chart, every venue you visited, AI-generated weekly insights powered by Groq. Not generic wellness tips. Patterns from your actual data.

And there's a pattern detection engine running in the background — a Supabase Edge Function that warns you when you're about to visit a place that's triggered you before. No LLM. Pure SQL. Fast, private, and it works offline.

---

[THE BUILD — 1:40–2:10]

Now here's where Kiro comes in.

We didn't just use AI to write code. We built a custom AI development system first.

A three-agent architecture: a conductor that orchestrates, a librarian that researches, and a development agent that implements. Every agent has a strict job. The conductor reads at most 3 files per task — everything else gets delegated. A 2-strike circuit breaker stops agents from patching symptoms instead of fixing root causes.

Before writing a single line of app code, Kiro ran our full Prompt-Driven Development process — 6 steps, from raw idea to 1,200-line design doc. Requirements, architecture, security, GDPR compliance for health data, neurodivergent UX guidelines — all researched, all documented, all committed to GitHub automatically.

The entire Supabase backend — 10 tables, 15 RLS policies, 4 Edge Functions, seed data for 15 real venues — was built in a single session. The TypeScript types were auto-generated from the live schema. The handoff doc for our second developer was written by Kiro after reading the existing code.

We didn't vibe code our way to a prototype. We engineered a system that builds software the right way.

---

[SOCIAL GOOD — 2:10–2:40]

Let's be clear about who this is for.

Autism. ADHD. PTSD. Sensory processing disorder. Anxiety. Migraine. These aren't edge cases — they're hundreds of millions of people who navigate a world that wasn't designed for them.

Sensly gives them something they've never had: real data about real places, from people who understand exactly what they're dealing with.

The recommendation engine uses a weighted penalty system built from published clinical research — NIH, Frontiers in Psychiatry, Cleveland Clinic. The diagnosis data is GDPR Article 9 compliant. It never touches the LLM layer. Hard delete on account deletion.

This isn't a wellness app with a pastel color scheme. It's infrastructure for a community that needs it.

---

[CLOSE — 2:40–3:00]

Sensly. Sensory insights, simply.

Built with Kiro. Backed by research. Designed for the people who need it most.

The map gets better every time someone uses it.

So does the world.

---

END OF SCRIPT
=============
