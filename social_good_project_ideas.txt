1. HEALTHCARE & TELEMEDICINE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1  OpenMRS — Open-Source Medical Records System
     Description: A community-driven, open-source electronic medical records
     (EMR) platform designed for resource-constrained environments. It allows
     clinics in low-income countries to digitize patient records, track
     treatments, and coordinate care without expensive proprietary software.
     Potential Impact: Improves continuity of care for millions of patients in
     Africa, Asia, and Latin America where paper records are still common.
     Tech Stack: Java, Spring, React; modular plugin architecture; REST API.
     Source: https://openmrs.org/the-emr4all-journey/

1.2  IntelehHealth — Last-Mile Telemedicine Platform
     Description: A telemedicine platform purpose-built for community health
     workers in rural and underserved areas. It enables remote consultations
     between frontline workers and doctors, supporting diagnosis and treatment
     guidance without requiring patients to travel long distances.
     Potential Impact: Extends specialist care to populations with no local
     access to physicians, reducing preventable deaths from treatable conditions.
     Tech Stack: Android mobile app, low-bandwidth optimization, cloud backend.
     Source: https://intelehealth.org/about-us/

1.3  Rural Telehealth Network Platform
     Description: A provider-to-provider telehealth coordination system that
     connects rural healthcare teams with urban specialists using audiovisual
     platforms. The model focuses on "moving knowledge, not patients," reducing
     costly and burdensome patient transfers for consultations.
     Potential Impact: Reduces healthcare disparities between rural and urban
     populations; lowers costs for patients and health systems.
     Tech Stack: WebRTC video, EHR integration APIs, HIPAA-compliant cloud.
     Source: https://www.ruralhealthinfo.org/topics/health-information-technology/project-examples

1.4  Awesome Healthcare Open Source Toolkit
     Description: A curated collection of open-source healthcare software,
     libraries, and tools covering EMRs, imaging, clinical decision support,
     and health data standards. Serves as a launchpad for developers building
     health tech for nonprofits and NGOs.
     Potential Impact: Lowers the barrier for NGOs and small clinics to adopt
     modern health IT without licensing costs.
     Tech Stack: Various (FHIR, HL7, DICOM, Python, JavaScript).
     Source: https://github.com/kakoni/awesome-healthcare

2. EDUCATION & DIGITAL LITERACY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1  Offline-First Learning Platform for Low-Connectivity Schools
     Description: An educational app designed to work fully offline, syncing
     content when connectivity is available. Targets students in low-income
     communities and developing regions where internet access is unreliable or
     absent. Curriculum can be localized and customized by teachers.
     Potential Impact: UNESCO estimates that improving basic reading skills in
     low-income countries could help 171 million people escape extreme poverty.
     Tech Stack: Progressive Web App (PWA), IndexedDB, service workers,
     lightweight CMS for content management.
     Source: https://ssir.org/articles/entry/removing_barriers_to_quality_education_in_the_mena_region

2.2  AWS Education Equity Initiative — Cloud Skills for Underserved Learners
     Description: A program (and replicable model) providing cloud computing
     and AI skills training to underserved learners globally. Organizations
     can build on this model to create free, self-paced digital skills courses
     targeting youth in low-income communities.
     Potential Impact: Bridges the digital skills gap, opening pathways to
     higher-paying tech careers for marginalized populations.
     Tech Stack: Cloud-based LMS, AI-assisted tutoring, multilingual content.
     Source: https://aws.amazon.com/about-aws/our-impact/education-equity-initiative/

2.3  EdTech Platform for Marginalized Learners
     Description: A mobile-first education platform designed with disaggregated
     user research to serve the most marginalized learners — including girls,
     children with disabilities, and those in conflict zones. Emphasizes
     inclusive design, local language support, and low data usage.
     Potential Impact: Addresses learning gaps exacerbated by COVID-19 and
     ongoing crises; reaches children who fall through the cracks of standard
     EdTech solutions.
     Tech Stack: Android/iOS, SMS fallback, multilingual NLP, offline sync.
     Source: https://www.ictworks.org/edutech-reach-most-marginalised-learners/

2.4  Teacher Monitoring & Training Information System
     Description: A technology-powered system for supporting, motivating, and
     training teachers at scale in low-resource settings. Uses data dashboards
     and mobile tools to give school administrators real-time insight into
     teacher performance and professional development needs.
     Potential Impact: Improves teaching quality in under-resourced schools,
     with downstream benefits for student outcomes and community development.
     Tech Stack: Mobile data collection (ODK/KoboToolbox), analytics dashboard,
     SMS notifications.
     Source: https://www.povertyactionlab.org/initiative-project/technology-powered-monitoring-and-information-system-supporting-motivating-and

3. ENVIRONMENT & CLIMATE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1  Open Sustainability Tech Ecosystem
     Description: A collection of open-source tools and platforms that support
     the transition to a sustainable economy — including carbon accounting
     software, energy modeling tools, and supply chain transparency platforms.
     Helps organizations make traceable, evidence-based sustainability decisions
     and prevents greenwashing.
     Potential Impact: Accelerates decarbonization by making sustainability
     tools accessible to small businesses, NGOs, and governments.
     Tech Stack: Python data pipelines, open data standards, REST APIs,
     geospatial libraries (GDAL, PostGIS).
     Source: https://opensustain.tech/blog/the_open_source_sustainability_ecosystem/

3.2  InaSafe — Natural Disaster Impact Assessment Tool
     Description: An open-source GIS plugin (originally built for Indonesia)
     that helps governments and NGOs assess the potential impact of natural
     disasters such as floods, earthquakes, and volcanic eruptions on
     populations and infrastructure. Now usable globally.
     Potential Impact: Enables evidence-based disaster preparedness planning,
     helping communities allocate resources before disasters strike.
     Tech Stack: QGIS plugin, Python, OpenStreetMap data, geospatial analysis.
     Source: https://www.techtarget.com/searchdisasterrecovery/tip/Open-source-emergency-management-tools-for-DR-teams

3.3  Community Air Quality Monitoring Network
     Description: A low-cost, open-hardware sensor network that allows
     communities — especially those near industrial zones — to monitor local
     air quality in real time. Data is published openly so residents and
     advocates can hold polluters accountable.
     Potential Impact: Empowers environmental justice communities with data
     to support advocacy, policy change, and health protection.
     Tech Stack: IoT sensors (Raspberry Pi / Arduino), MQTT, time-series DB
     (InfluxDB), public-facing map dashboard.
     Source: https://www.unep.org/topics/digital-transformations/sustainable-digitalization

3.4  Digitalization for Sustainability (UNEP Framework)
     Description: A framework and set of digital tools promoted by the UN
     Environment Programme to proactively use technology for environmental
     targets — including satellite monitoring of deforestation, ocean health
     tracking, and biodiversity mapping.
     Potential Impact: Provides governments and NGOs with actionable
     environmental intelligence to meet climate commitments.
     Tech Stack: Satellite imagery (Sentinel/Landsat), ML classification,
     cloud GIS platforms.
     Source: https://www.unep.org/topics/digital-transformations/sustainable-digitalization

4. DISASTER RELIEF & HUMANITARIAN RESPONSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1  Sahana Eden — Open-Source Disaster Management System
     Description: A free, open-source emergency management platform developed
     by the Sahana Software Foundation. It coordinates disaster response
     between governments, aid organizations, and civil society — managing
     missing persons registries, shelter tracking, volunteer coordination,
     and supply logistics.
     Potential Impact: Has been deployed in responses to major disasters
     including the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami and the Haiti earthquake,
     saving lives through better coordination.
     Tech Stack: Python (web2py), PostgreSQL, REST API, mobile-friendly UI.
     Source: https://sahanafoundation.org/about/

4.2  AIDR — AI-Powered Disaster Response Social Media Classifier
     Description: A free, open platform that uses human-machine intelligence
     to filter and classify social media messages during emergencies and
     humanitarian crises. Can automatically tag thousands of messages per
     minute to surface actionable information for responders.
     Potential Impact: Dramatically speeds up situational awareness during
     crises, helping responders prioritize where help is needed most.
     Tech Stack: Python, NLP/ML classifiers, Twitter/social media APIs,
     crowdsourcing interface.
     Source: https://aidr.qcri.org/

4.3  Ushahidi — Crisis Mapping Platform
     Description: An open-source platform that allows volunteers and
     organizations to collect, visualize, and map reports from the field
     during humanitarian crises. Originally built to map post-election
     violence in Kenya, it is now used globally for disaster response,
     human rights monitoring, and community reporting.
     Potential Impact: Democratizes crisis reporting, giving affected
     communities a voice and providing responders with ground-truth data.
     Tech Stack: PHP/Laravel backend, React frontend, geospatial mapping
     (Leaflet/OpenStreetMap), SMS/email/social media ingestion.
     Source: https://ssir.org/articles/entry/open_source_for_humanitarian_action

4.4  HeiGIT Open-Source Geospatial Disaster Tools
     Description: A suite of open-source geographic tools built on
     OpenStreetMap data to support disaster and risk management — including
     routing for emergency vehicles, population exposure analysis, and
     infrastructure vulnerability mapping.
     Potential Impact: Provides humanitarian organizations with free,
     community-maintained geographic intelligence for disaster preparedness
     and response.
     Tech Stack: OpenStreetMap, Java/Python, ohsome API, QGIS integration.
     Source: https://heigit.org/five-open-source-tools-to-harness-geographic-knowledge-for-disaster-management/

5. CIVIC TECH, DEMOCRACY & TRANSPARENCY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1  Open Source Election Technology (OSET Institute)
     Description: A nonprofit initiative building publicly-owned, open-source
     election technology to replace aging, proprietary voting systems. The
     goal is verifiable, accurate, secure, and transparent elections through
     software that any jurisdiction can audit and deploy.
     Potential Impact: Rebuilds public trust in elections by making the
     technology fully auditable and removing dependence on opaque vendors.
     Tech Stack: Open-source voting software stack, cryptographic audit
     trails, accessible ballot design.
     Source: https://www.osetinstitute.org/

5.2  Vote Monitor — Election Observation App
     Description: An open-source mobile app that enables citizens and
     accredited observers to report election irregularities before, during,
     and after Election Day. Reports are aggregated by local NGOs leading
     long-term observation efforts.
     Potential Impact: Strengthens democratic accountability by crowdsourcing
     election monitoring at a scale impossible with traditional observer teams.
     Tech Stack: React Native mobile app, REST API, real-time dashboard,
     open data export.
     Source: https://commitglobal.org/en/democracy-transparency

5.3  Civic Tech DC / Code for America Model
     Description: A volunteer-driven civic tech model (exemplified by groups
     like Civic Tech DC and Code for America brigades) that builds open-source
     tools for local government and community needs — from benefits eligibility
     screeners to public transit apps to court date reminder systems.
     Potential Impact: Makes government services more accessible and reduces
     barriers for vulnerable populations navigating complex bureaucracies.
     Tech Stack: Varies by project; typically JavaScript/Python web apps,
     open government data APIs.
     Source: https://www.civictechdc.org/about

5.4  SecureDrop — Whistleblower Platform
     Description: An open-source, anonymous submission system for
     whistleblowers to securely share documents and tips with journalists.
     Developed by Freedom of the Press Foundation and used by major news
     organizations worldwide.
     Potential Impact: Protects sources and enables investigative journalism
     that holds powerful institutions accountable.
     Tech Stack: Tor network, air-gapped servers, Python/Flask, end-to-end
     encryption.
     Source: https://www.fordfoundation.org/news-and-stories/big-ideas/public-interest-technology/

6. ACCESSIBILITY & DISABILITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1  Disability-Inclusive Climate Preparedness App
     Description: A mobile application that provides personalized emergency
     preparedness guidance for people with disabilities during extreme weather
     events. Research shows the mortality rate for disabled people in natural
     disasters is four times higher than for non-disabled people, making
     targeted tools critical.
     Potential Impact: Reduces preventable deaths and injuries among the 16%
     of the global population living with disabilities during climate disasters.
     Tech Stack: React Native, accessible UI (WCAG 2.1 AA), location services,
     push notifications, offline mode.
     Source: https://www.perkins.org/5-disabilitytech-companies-prioritizing-accessibility-and-safety-amid-climate-change/

6.2  Sensing Climate — Disability-Led Climate Research Platform
     Description: A research and technology project placing disabled people
     at the forefront of climate crisis navigation. Embeds the expertise of
     disabled people in new strategies and tools for coping with heat waves,
     floods, and other climate disruptions.
     Potential Impact: Produces inclusive climate adaptation strategies that
     serve the most vulnerable populations rather than designing them out.
     Tech Stack: Participatory design methods, sensor data collection,
     qualitative data platforms.
     Source: https://www.ecehh.org/research/disability-inclusive-climate-action/

6.3  Low-Emission, Accessible Web Design Framework
     Description: A design system and toolkit for building websites that are
     simultaneously low in carbon emissions and highly accessible. Research
     from ACM Interactions shows that reducing a website's environmental
     footprint (fewer heavy assets, faster loads) also benefits users with
     slow connections or limited data plans.
     Potential Impact: Serves both environmental and social equity goals;
     makes the web more inclusive for people in low-bandwidth regions.
     Tech Stack: CSS/HTML best practices, performance budgeting tools,
     automated accessibility testing (axe, Lighthouse).
     Source: https://interactions.acm.org/blog/view/exploring-parallels-between-sustainability-and-social-access-in-hci

7. POVERTY ALLEVIATION & FINANCIAL INCLUSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

7.1  Digital Tools for Underbanked Populations
     Description: Mobile-first financial services platforms (digital wallets,
     micro-savings, micro-lending) designed for people without access to
     traditional banking. New technologies break down barriers to financial
     services for the estimated 1.4 billion unbanked adults worldwide.
     Potential Impact: Enables savings, credit access, and economic
     participation for populations historically excluded from formal finance.
     Tech Stack: Mobile money APIs (M-Pesa model), biometric identity
     verification, offline transaction support.
     Source: https://www.kiplinger.com/kiplinger-advisor-collective/digital-tools-for-underbanked-populations

7.2  mPowering — Incentive-Based Behavior Change App
     Description: An app that awards goods and services to individuals facing
     extreme poverty when they make beneficial choices — such as attending
     school, seeking prenatal care, or completing vaccination schedules.
     Combines conditional cash transfer logic with mobile technology.
     Potential Impact: Addresses multiple dimensions of poverty simultaneously
     by incentivizing health and education behaviors.
     Tech Stack: Android app, SMS fallback, digital voucher system, NGO
     partnership integration.
     Source: https://ssir.org/articles/entry/whats_next_antipoverty_apps

7.3  UNICEF Startup Lab — Tech for Children's Development
     Description: A program supporting tech-enabled startups addressing
     critical development challenges in education, health, WASH (water,
     sanitation, hygiene), agriculture, financial inclusion, disability, and
     climate action — with a focus on improving children's lives in Ghana
     and similar contexts.
     Potential Impact: Scales proven social impact models across multiple
     SDG areas simultaneously, with a focus on the most vulnerable children.
     Tech Stack: Varies by startup; mobile-first, low-bandwidth, local
     language support emphasized.
     Source: https://www.unicef.org/ghana/press-releases/unicef-startup-lab-2024-demo-day-25-tech-enabled-social-impact-startups-present

8. MENTAL HEALTH & SOCIAL ISOLATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

8.1  AI-Assisted Mental Health Screening Tool
     Description: A purpose-built AI application for reducing specific
     symptoms of anxiety and depression, tracking relapse risks (e.g., for
     psychosis), and encouraging preventative behavior changes. Designed to
     complement — not replace — professional care, especially in areas with
     mental health provider shortages.
     Potential Impact: Extends mental health support to underserved populations
     who cannot access or afford traditional therapy.
     Tech Stack: NLP/ML models, conversational AI, secure data storage,
     clinical validation framework.
     Source: https://post.parliament.uk/research-briefings/post-pn-0737/

8.2  Joy App — Social Prescribing Platform
     Description: A web-based platform that digitizes and streamlines the
     process of connecting people to non-medical community services — covering
     social isolation, loneliness, mental health support, and social welfare.
     Integrates with 90% of GP systems in England to enable "social
     prescribing" at scale.
     Potential Impact: Reduces pressure on clinical services by connecting
     patients to community resources; addresses the loneliness epidemic.
     Tech Stack: Web app, EHR/GP system APIs, community resource database,
     referral workflow engine.
     Source: https://www.plymouth.ac.uk/research/centre-for-health-technology/case-studies/the-joy-app

8.3  Grief Support Platform with Generative AI
     Description: A CITRIS-funded student project combining generative AI,
     spatial audio for emotional immersion, and human-AI collaboration to
     provide grief support. Designed to offer compassionate, accessible
     support for people experiencing loss who may not seek traditional therapy.
     Potential Impact: Addresses an underserved mental health need at scale,
     particularly for isolated individuals or those in communities with
     stigma around grief counseling.
     Tech Stack: Generative AI (LLM), spatial audio, human-in-the-loop
     design, web/mobile interface.
     Source: https://news.ucsc.edu/2025/01/citris-student-projects.html

8.4  Beluga Pods — Anti-Loneliness Social Platform
     Description: A multi-faceted social media and wellness platform designed
     to reduce loneliness among users. Focuses on building genuine community
     connections rather than engagement-maximizing algorithms that can worsen
     mental health outcomes.
     Potential Impact: Addresses the global loneliness epidemic, which the
     WHO has identified as a major public health threat.
     Tech Stack: Social platform architecture, community matching algorithms,
     wellness check-in features, privacy-first design.
     Source: https://www.plymouth.ac.uk/research/centre-for-health-technology/case-studies/beluga-pods

9. FOOD SECURITY & AGRICULTURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

9.1  AI-Powered Food Insecurity Prediction System
     Description: A machine learning system used by NGOs to predict food
     insecurity and population movement using satellite data, weather patterns,
     and socioeconomic indicators. Enables proactive humanitarian response
     before crises become acute.
     Potential Impact: Allows aid organizations to pre-position resources and
     intervene earlier, reducing the severity of food crises.
     Tech Stack: Satellite imagery analysis, time-series ML models, geospatial
     data pipelines, NGO operations dashboards.
     Source: https://www.omdena.com/blog/innovative-ngos-future-of-ai-for-good

9.2  Personalized Healthy Food Choice App for Low-Income Users
     Description: A supermarket and food-choice app that personalizes
     recommendations to promote healthier eating, with special attention to
     people in lower socioeconomic positions who face structural barriers to
     healthy food access. Research-backed design from Tilburg University.
     Potential Impact: Reduces diet-related chronic disease disparities between
     income groups; supports food equity goals.
     Tech Stack: Mobile app, recommendation engine, behavioral nudge design,
     integration with grocery store APIs.
     Source: https://www.tilburguniversity.edu/magazine/overview/icon-projects-digital-sciences-society

9.3  Indigenous Food Systems Sustainability Platform
     Description: A technology platform supporting sustainable food systems
     for Native American tribes, combining traditional ecological knowledge
     with modern data tools to manage land, water, and food resources in
     the face of climate change.
     Potential Impact: Strengthens food sovereignty and ecosystem health for
     Indigenous communities disproportionately affected by climate disruption.
     Tech Stack: GIS mapping, community data governance tools, mobile data
     collection, open data standards.
     Source: https://health.ucdavis.edu/nursing/Research/distinctions/adapt_tech.html

10. AI FOR SOCIAL GOOD (CROSS-DOMAIN)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

10.1 Google AI for Social Good Program
     Description: Google's initiative supporting 30+ AI projects addressing
     social challenges across health, environment, education, and crisis
     response. Provides funding, technical resources, and Google AI expertise
     to nonprofits and researchers building impactful AI applications.
     Potential Impact: Accelerates AI adoption in the social sector by
     lowering technical and financial barriers for mission-driven organizations.
     Tech Stack: TensorFlow, Google Cloud AI, Vertex AI, open datasets.
     Source: https://www.blog.google/technology/ai/30-new-ai-for-social-good-projects/

10.2 AI for Wildlife Conservation (Earth Species Project)
     Description: A nonprofit using AI to decode non-human communication —
     including sperm whale vocalizations — to advance wildlife conservation.
     By understanding animal communication, conservationists can better
     protect ecosystems and biodiversity.
     Potential Impact: Opens new frontiers in conservation science; builds
     public empathy for wildlife through scientific discovery.
     Tech Stack: Bioacoustics ML models, large audio datasets, Python
     (PyTorch/TensorFlow), open research publication.
     Source: https://ssir.org/articles/entry/ai-nonprofits-climate

10.3 AI for Humanitarian Operations (Omdena / NGO Deployments)
     Description: Practical AI systems deployed within NGO operations —
     including predictive analytics for refugee movement, satellite-based
     damage assessments after disasters, and operational planning tools.
     These are production systems, not research prototypes.
     Potential Impact: Transforms humanitarian organizations from reactive
     to proactive, improving efficiency and outcomes at scale.
     Tech Stack: Python ML pipelines, satellite imagery APIs, operational
     dashboards, human-in-the-loop validation.
     Source: https://www.omdena.com/blog/innovative-ngos-future-of-ai-for-good

10.4 AI-Powered Nonprofit Landscape (SSIR Research)
     Description: A growing ecosystem of nonprofits using AI for social
     impact — from legal aid chatbots to climate modeling to accessibility
     tools. The Stanford Social Innovation Review has mapped this landscape
     to help funders and social entrepreneurs identify opportunities and
     avoid duplication.
     Potential Impact: Helps the social sector learn from each other and
     direct AI investment toward the highest-impact applications.
     Tech Stack: Varies; LLMs, computer vision, predictive analytics,
     NLP — applied to social sector use cases.
     Source: https://ssir.org/articles/entry/ai-powered-nonprofits-landscape
